urlLink aglet. The American Heritage Dictionary of the English Language: Fourth Edition. 2000. : Aglet SYLLABICATION:aglet PRONUNCIATION:  glt  NOUN:1. A tag or sheath, as of plastic, on the end of a lace, cord, or ribbon to facilitate its passing through eyelet holes. 2. A similar device used for an ornament.   ETYMOLOGY:Middle English, from Old French aguillette, diminutive of aguille, needle, from Vulgar Latin *accula, from Late Latin acucula, diminutive of Latin acus, needle. See ak- in Appendix I. "
